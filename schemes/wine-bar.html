Six rooms. Six dinners. A study in the architecture of attention by an architect who will not stop looking at corners.

by josh harrison · photographs by self · 24 min read

The corner is not the wall meeting the wall. It is the wall agreeing to meet.

Most architecture writing wants you to look up. This is about looking down — at the threshold, the joint, the place where the room admits it had to start somewhere. The corner is the room's first decision. Everything else is consequence.

I have been building a log of these decisions for two years. What follows is a sample.
