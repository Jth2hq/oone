# OONE — Brand Voice Guidelines

_Derived from every attached variation. Voice is unusually consistent
across the set — this document codifies what's already there._

## The one-line

**OONE** is the personal practice of Josh Harrison — architect,
designer, image-maker — working _in itself, in orbit_ between rooms,
objects, and interfaces that pay attention to edges, thresholds, and
the long quiet of a surface.

## Tonal vector

```
  quiet ───────────────────────◆────────── loud
  technical ──────◆──────────────────────── poetic
  modern ────────────────◆────────────────── archival
  cool ─────────────◆───────────────────── warm
  selling ─────────────────────────────◆── witnessing
```

OONE witnesses. It does not pitch.

## Voice pillars

1. **Architectural, not architectural-speak.**
   Talk about surfaces, pressures, thresholds, weather — not about
   "design language" or "solutions." Trust the reader to feel the
   room.

2. **Italicized clauses carry the weight.**
   The important half of every sentence sits in italic amber. The
   rest is scaffolding. A sentence without an italic clause is
   probably underwritten.

3. **Small units. Handheld.**
   Max ~24 words per sentence on body. Headlines fit on a breath.
   Mono labels are ≤ 5 words.

4. **No announcements.**
   Never "introducing," "discover," "explore." The work is here; the
   reader has already arrived.

5. **Data as poetry.**
   Percentages, coordinates, ordinals, dates in `mmxxvi`. They're
   ornament as much as information. Keep them accurate.

## Sentence templates

- `{noun} is not {shape} but a {pressure}` — the room / threshold test.
- `a practice of {edges}` — studio self-description.
- `{material}, {material}, {material}` — triads name a scheme.
- `attention is {architecture}` — the manifesto cadence.

## Copy patterns by surface

### Wordmark
`o o n e` — the second `o` is italic + amber. Always lowercase in body
type; the loader may render display-sized with the same construction.

### Tagline
`in itself, in orbit` — appears as hero subtitle or meta line. The
comma matters.

### Section labels (mono)
`§ note`, `§ manifesto`, `§ studio`, `filed under —`, `by arrangement only`.

### Inscriptions (weathered into the field)
- `rooms that remember`
- `thresholds hold` _breath_
- `the long quiet`
- `of a surface` _just touched_

### Fragments (whispered in corners)
- `attention is` _architecture_ `— what we look at longer becomes the room.`
- `a` _threshold_ `is the weather on either side of it.`
- `some surfaces` _remember_ `. concrete, velvet, breath on glass.`
- `the body passes through and the` _room keeps_ `a little of it.`

### Program rows
Each row: `ordinal · title · ingredients`
  - `i — Steam Room Study — cedar, eucalyptus, fog`
  - `ii — Powder Room Tour — glass, rose, silver`

### Contact
Preferred: `by arrangement only` → `studio@oone.work`.
Never "hire me," "book a call."

The entry text: _in itself, in orbit_ — centered on the object.

### Scheme B · Steam Mirror

Re-fog notice (after 6s idle): `the room is steamed again`
Hidden reveal near bottom-right: `by arrangement only —
studio@oone.work`

## Accessibility

Reduce-motion media query disables the breath, orbit, drip, and fog
animations. Copy remains legible against paper or vellum with AA
contrast at the body weights. All italic amber clauses are wrapped
in `<em>` so they survive unstyled rendering.
